# Best Poetry Models (April 2026): A Survey for Singulars / ground.exe

**Author:** Research analyst note for Halim Madi
**Date:** 30 April 2026
**Purpose:** Pick (a) closed-source models to benchmark community-voted poems against, and (b) open-weight bases for fine-tuning `ground.exe`.

---

## 1. Methodology — what to trust, what to discount

Three classes of evidence are available right now, and they disagree more than the marketing suggests:

1. **Crowd preference leaderboards** — primarily [LMArena (formerly Chatbot Arena)](https://lmarena.ai/leaderboard/text/hard-prompts), now at 354 models and ~6.0M votes as of 29 April 2026 ([leaderboard page](https://arena.ai/leaderboard/text)). LMArena's Creative Writing category was built using a Llama-3.1-70B-Instruct classifier that hit 96.1% labelling accuracy against GPT-4o-mini gold labels and was cross-checked against Claude 3.5 Sonnet ([category methodology](https://news.lmarena.ai/arena-category/)). Useful, but it measures _median user preference on chat-style prompts_, not poetic craft. Voters reward fluency, helpfulness, and completion of the brief. Poetry that takes risks or breaks form will lose to competent middle-of-the-road verse.

2. **LLM-judged craft benchmarks** — chiefly [EQ-Bench Creative Writing v3](https://eqbench.com/creative_writing.html) and its sibling [Longform Creative Writing](https://eqbench.com/creative_writing_longform.html). v3 runs 32 prompts × 3 iterations, uses a hybrid rubric + Elo (pairwise) system with Claude Sonnet 4 as judge, and ships sub-metrics for Slop, Repetition, Style, Length and a per-ability radar ([about page](https://eqbench.com/about.html#creative-writing-v3); [GitHub](https://github.com/EQ-bench/creative-writing-bench)). EQ-Bench also publishes an explicit [Slop Score](https://eqbench.com/slop-score.html) (60% slop words, 25% "not just X but Y" patterns, 15% slop trigrams) — the closest public proxy for "AI-poetry stink". Caveat: any benchmark whose judge is a frontier model is biased toward the family the judge was trained in; Anthropic-judged benchmarks tend to flatter Anthropic models, and EQ-Bench's authors openly acknowledge this risk ([Judgemark v2.1](https://eqbench.com/judgemark-v2.html)).

3. **Vendor decks and "best-AI-for-writing" round-ups** — basically all SEO. I cite a few for pricing and pull-quotes (e.g. [Vellum](https://www.vellum.ai/blog/claude-opus-4-7-benchmarks-explained), [BenchLM](https://benchlm.ai/blog/posts/claude-api-pricing), [glbgpt](https://www.glbgpt.com/hub/gpt-5-4-pricing/), [pricepertoken](https://pricepertoken.com/pricing-page/model/openai-gpt-5)) but their qualitative claims should be discounted heavily.

**My triangulation rule:** a model only counts as a real creative-writing contender if it places top-10 on **both** LMArena Creative Writing **and** EQ-Bench Creative Writing v3, and its Slop Score is in the lower (better) half of the table. That eliminates models that are "popular" but slop-heavy (most GPT-family models pre-5.5) and models that judges love but humans don't (some Claude variants tuned to please Claude judges).

I also explicitly discount: Klu, llm-stats, BenchLM aggregate "writing scores" — they re-package the two leaderboards above with opaque weighting. And I discount any blog claim that doesn't reduce to one of those two underlying sources. There is no third independent poetry benchmark with statistical power right now; the field disagrees most loudly on **whether judge-bias invalidates EQ-Bench's Anthropic-leaning rankings** — see the discrepancy in [recent reporting](https://eqbench.com/creative_writing.html), where March 2026 had Sonnet 4.6 on top at 1936 Elo and April 2026 has Opus 4.7 at 2216 Elo, vs. LMArena where Gemini 3.1 Pro leads creative writing by 18 Elo points over Opus 4.6 ([arena.ai](https://arena.ai/leaderboard/text), [evy.so aggregation](https://evy.so/compare/best-llms-for-writing/)).

For Singulars specifically, the **community vote** (humans judging humans-vs-machine, blind) is the only ground truth that matters for the project's narrative. The benchmarks above just narrow the candidate pool.

## 2. State of the field — April 2026

**Closed-source frontier.** As of late April 2026 the four labs that matter for poetry are Anthropic (Claude Opus 4.7, Sonnet 4.6, Haiku 4.5), OpenAI (GPT-5.5 standard and Pro tiers, GPT-5.4, GPT-4o legacy), Google (Gemini 3.1 Pro, Gemini 3 Flash, Gemini 2.5 Pro), and xAI (Grok 4.1 Thinking).

- **Claude Opus 4.7** leads EQ-Bench Creative Writing v3 at 2216 Elo, with GPT-5.5 second at 2024 and Claude Sonnet 4.6 third at 1991 ([eqbench.com](https://eqbench.com/creative_writing.html)). On LMArena it is, however, _not_ the top creative writer — Gemini 3.1 Pro holds #1 with 1487 Elo, Gemini 3 Flash is at 1461, and GPT-5.4 High is 9th ([arena.ai/leaderboard/text](https://arena.ai/leaderboard/text)). The boring-but-important caveat about Opus 4.7: independent reviewers ([MindStudio](https://www.mindstudio.ai/blog/claude-opus-47-review-whats-new), [boringbot Substack](https://boringbot.substack.com/p/claude-opus-47-heres-what-works-and)) note that creative writing did **not** meaningfully improve over 4.6, and several writers report 4.7 reaches more for bullet points and headers and less for flowing prose. Opus 4.7 also ships a new tokenizer that can produce up to 35% more tokens for the same input, raising effective cost ([Finout](https://www.finout.io/blog/claude-opus-4.7-pricing-the-real-cost-story-behind-the-unchanged-price-tag)).

- **GPT-5.5** (released 23 April 2026, [OpenAI](https://openai.com/index/introducing-gpt-5-5/)) is OpenAI's first frontier release where prose noticeably tightened. EQ-Bench has it #2. Pricing: $5/M input, $30/M output for standard; $30/$180 for Pro ([OpenRouter](https://openrouter.ai/openai/gpt-5.5), [Apidog](https://apidog.com/blog/gpt-5-5-pricing/)). The earlier GPT-5 (Aug 2025) was $1.25/$10 ([pricepertoken](https://pricepertoken.com/pricing-page/model/openai-gpt-5)) and is largely deprecated for serious writing tasks.

- **Gemini 3.1 Pro** is the LMArena Creative Writing champion as of April 2026 ([arena.ai](https://arena.ai/leaderboard/text)). At $2/M input and $12/M output below 200K context, it is by far the cheapest of the frontier triumvirate ([NxCode pricing guide](https://www.nxcode.io/resources/news/gemini-3-1-pro-complete-guide-benchmarks-pricing-api-2026)). Crowd-pleasing prose, somewhat less distinct voice than Claude per [tactiq.io](https://tactiq.io/learn/claude-vs-gemini-vs-chatgpt-for-writing).

- **Grok 4.1 Thinking** appears as #1 on the [llm-stats Creative Writing v3 aggregator](https://llm-stats.com/benchmarks/creative-writing-v3) at 1721.9 (29 April 2026) — but llm-stats normalises differently than EQ-Bench itself. Treat as "strong contender, judges disagree".

**Open-weight frontier.** The four families that matter for fine-tuning a poetry model are:

- **DeepSeek V3.2 / R1.** All weights open under permissive licence ([api-docs.deepseek.com](https://api-docs.deepseek.com/quick_start/pricing), [HuggingFace org](https://huggingface.co/deepseek-ai)). API is the cheapest of any serious model: V3.2 chat is $0.28/M input, $0.42/M output; R1 is $0.55/$2.19 ([NxCode](https://www.nxcode.io/resources/news/deepseek-api-pricing-complete-guide-2026)). EQ-Bench Creative Writing v3 anchors its Elo scale at deepseek-r1 = 1500 — i.e. R1 is the explicit reference point everything else is calibrated against ([github](https://github.com/EQ-bench/creative-writing-bench)).

- **Qwen3 family.** Alibaba ships Qwen3-235B-A22B (MoE, 235B total / 22B active), Qwen3-32B, Qwen3-14B and smaller, all on HuggingFace under **Apache 2.0** ([Qwen3-235B-A22B-Instruct-2507](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen3-235B-A22B-Instruct-2507), [licence](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen3-235B-A22B-Instruct-2507/blob/main/LICENSE)). Apache 2.0 is the cleanest licence in the frontier-class group — no research-only restrictions, fine to deploy commercially. Qwen3 explicitly tunes for "human preference alignment in creative writing, role-playing, and multi-turn dialogues" per the [Qwen team blog](https://qwenlm.github.io/blog/qwen3/), and SiliconFlow's [open-source-LLM-for-creative-writing-2026 round-up](https://www.siliconflow.com/articles/en/best-open-source-llm-for-creative-writing-ideation) names Qwen3-235B-A22B, DeepSeek V3, and Qwen3-14B as the top three open creative-writing bases.

- **Llama 3.3 70B Instruct.** Meta's last "small-flagship" before Llama 4. Matches Llama 3.1 405B's quality at a fraction of the cost; +9.2 on MATH and 92.1 on IFEval over 3.1 ([insiderllm](https://insiderllm.com/guides/llama-3-guide-every-size/)). The relevant fine-tune precedent is [tdrussell/Llama-3-70B-Instruct-Storywriter](https://huggingface.co/tdrussell/Llama-3-70B-Instruct-Storywriter), trained with Rank-64 QLoRA on 800 fiction books, 8192-seq, on 4× RTX 4090. The author specifically notes the 70B base survives QLoRA fine-tuning on creative data while the 8B base "took a huge hit to intelligence". Confirmed reproducibility on Llama 3.3 70B with PyTorch FSDP + Q-LoRA ([Phil Schmid guide](https://www.philschmid.de/fsdp-qlora-llama3)).

- **Mistral Large 2 / Mistral Nemo 12B.** Mistral Large 2 is open-weight on HuggingFace under the **Mistral Research License** — non-commercial; commercial use needs a paid licence ([mistralai/Mistral-Large-Instruct-2407](https://huggingface.co/mistralai/Mistral-Large-Instruct-2407)). Fine-tunable via [mistral-finetune](https://github.com/mistralai/mistral-finetune). Mistral Nemo 12B is Apache 2.0 and has a thriving creative-writing fine-tune ecosystem on HuggingFace: [nbeerbower/mistral-nemo-gutenberg-12B](https://huggingface.co/nbeerbower/mistral-nemo-gutenberg-12B), [Lyra-Gutenberg-mistral-nemo-12B](https://huggingface.co/nbeerbower/Lyra-Gutenberg-mistral-nemo-12B), and the RPMax series — all of which fine-tune on Project Gutenberg literary corpora and are praised by the community for prose feel.

**Poetry-specific HuggingFace fine-tunes.** None of the publicly listed poetry models are at frontier scale or have published human evals. The notable ones are: [ashiqabdulkhader/GPT2-Poet](https://huggingface.co/ashiqabdulkhader/GPT2-Poet) (English, GPT-2 base), [HooshvareLab/gpt2-fa-poetry](https://huggingface.co/HooshvareLab/gpt2-fa-poetry) (Persian), [8lianno/llama_poetry_fa](https://huggingface.co/8lianno/llama_poetry_fa) (Persian, Llama 3.1 base), [karbolak/llama3.2-qlora-finetunined-poetry-fusion](https://huggingface.co/karbolak/llama3.2-qlora-finetunined-poetry-fusion) (Llama 3.2 QLoRA), [Shriharsh/qwen3-0.6b-creative-writing](https://huggingface.co/Shriharsh/qwen3-0.6b-creative-writing). These are useful as data and recipe inspiration, not as benchmark contestants.

**Prior art worth referencing.**

- **Verse by Verse** (Google Research, 2020) — fine-tuned a per-poet model on Dickinson, Whitman, Poe etc., trained for "what verse best follows this verse" semantic prediction ([sites.research.google/versebyverse](https://sites.research.google/versebyverse/), [blog.google](https://blog.google/technology/ai/verse-by-verse/)). Pre-LLM, but conceptually the closest precedent for a "trained-on-a-style" approach.
- **Köbis & Mossink (2024)** — the [Nature _Scientific Reports_ study](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-024-76900-1) showing AI-generated poetry is _indistinguishable_ from human-written poetry to non-expert readers, and **rated more favourably**. Important framing for Singulars: the bar for "fooling humans" is genuinely low; the harder challenge is fooling poets.
- **PoetRAT** (Liu et al., 2026, _Engineering Applications of AI_) — RAG + chain-of-thought for classical Chinese poetry, with a Prosody Knowledge Base and a Poetry Techniques Knowledge Graph; reports >95% format-accuracy gains and 20–30% rhyme-accuracy gains without retraining ([sciencedirect.com](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1568494626005831)). Strongest published case for "small model + retrieval" beating brute-force scaling on formal poetry.
- **Jhamtani et al.'s LLM-based multi-agent poetry generation in non-cooperative environments** ([arxiv 2409.03659](https://arxiv.org/html/2409.03659v2)) — multi-agent setups produce more diverse and higher-rated poems than single-LLM baselines.
- **ICCC'25 (Campinas) and ICCC'26 (Coimbra)** ([computationalcreativity.net/iccc26](https://computationalcreativity.net/iccc26/)) — the canonical venue for computational-creativity poetry papers; worth grepping the ICCC'25 [full papers list](https://computationalcreativity.net/iccc25/full-papers/) for primary research.
- **Allison Parrish's "Language models can only write poetry"** ([decontextualize.com](https://posts.decontextualize.com/language-models-poetry/)) — the artist's reading worth citing in Singulars' philosophy section, irrespective of model choice.

## 4. Shortlist A — closed-source benchmark candidates (3)

The job here is _not_ to pick the model that will win — it's to pick three models that, taken together, will produce **maximally informative human votes**. They should differ in voice, in training-philosophy, and in price-tier so the Substack write-up has something to say.

1. **Claude Opus 4.7** — the EQ-Bench champion. If Halim's poets are losing to anything, it'll be this. Distinct voice, dense metaphor, occasional structural drift. The "highbrow" contestant. Worth running despite the [reported regression in long-form prose](https://boringbot.substack.com/p/claude-opus-47-heres-what-works-and) precisely _because_ the regression is part of the story. ([eqbench.com](https://eqbench.com/creative_writing.html), [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/claude/opus))

2. **Gemini 3.1 Pro** — the LMArena champion. Triangulates against Opus: Gemini wins crowd preference, Opus wins judge preference. Cheap enough ($2/$12) to run thousands of samples per theme. The "populist" contestant. ([arena.ai](https://arena.ai/leaderboard/text), [NxCode](https://www.nxcode.io/resources/news/gemini-3-1-pro-complete-guide-benchmarks-pricing-api-2026))

3. **DeepSeek R1** — the open-source spoiler. Ranks mid-table on benchmarks but is the EQ-Bench anchor model and costs ~10× less than Opus. If Halim's audience can't tell the difference between R1's poetry and Opus 4.7's, that's a much sharper story than "frontier model wins". Also — R1's weights are Halim's anyway, so it's a bridge between Shortlist A and Shortlist B. ([api-docs.deepseek.com](https://api-docs.deepseek.com/quick_start/pricing), [github](https://github.com/EQ-bench/creative-writing-bench))

I'm deliberately _not_ recommending GPT-5.5 in the top three. It performs well on EQ-Bench but is OpenAI's most expensive ($30/M output) and adds the least narrative differentiation against the other two — its voice sits between Claude and Gemini. Worth a side-by-side sanity check, not a slot.

## 5. Shortlist B — open-source fine-tuning bases for ground.exe (3)

The constraints: (i) Apache-2.0-or-similar commercial-friendly licence (so Halim can ship a HuggingFace model card and a Substack); (ii) fits a fine-tune on plausible hardware (≤8× H100, ideally a single 8×A100 or 4× RTX 4090 for QLoRA); (iii) precedent for surviving creative-writing fine-tunes without losing instruction-following.

1. **Qwen3-14B (Apache 2.0)** — the _top recommendation_. Big enough to hold a voice (per [SiliconFlow's open-source-LLM-for-creative-writing round-up](https://www.siliconflow.com/articles/en/best-open-source-llm-for-creative-writing-ideation), Qwen3-14B is their #3 pick alongside the 235B model and DeepSeek V3, specifically for "efficient creative performance at an affordable price"). Fits in QLoRA on a single A100 80GB; full LoRA on 2× A100. HuggingFace Trainer / Unsloth / LlamaFactory all support it day-one. Apache 2.0 means Halim can publish weights and a model card without licence drama. The 14B-class bet is also pragmatic: most of the published "fine-tune killed creativity" failure modes are at 8B and below. ([Qwen3 GitHub](https://github.com/QwenLM/Qwen3), [Qwen-32B card](https://huggingface.co/Qwen/Qwen3-32B))

2. **Llama 3.3 70B Instruct** — the _prestige bet_. The single most-validated fine-tune lineage for creative writing on HuggingFace: [tdrussell/Llama-3-70B-Instruct-Storywriter](https://huggingface.co/tdrussell/Llama-3-70B-Instruct-Storywriter) is the proof-of-recipe (Rank-64 QLoRA, 800 books, 8192 seq, 4× RTX 4090). Llama 3.3 70B inherits 405B-class quality at 70B cost. Hardware: QLoRA-able on 2× H100 80GB or 4× RTX 4090; FSDP+Q-LoRA recipe documented by [Phil Schmid](https://www.philschmid.de/fsdp-qlora-llama3). Licence: Llama 3 Community Licence — fine for Halim's traffic level (under the 700M-MAU threshold). ([insiderllm](https://insiderllm.com/guides/llama-3-guide-every-size/), [llama.com fine-tune guide](https://www.llama.com/docs/how-to-guides/fine-tuning/))

3. **Mistral Nemo 12B (Apache 2.0)** — the _cheapest, most artistically validated_ option. Despite being "only" 12B, it has the richest literary fine-tune ecosystem on HuggingFace: the Gutenberg series ([nbeerbower/mistral-nemo-gutenberg-12B](https://huggingface.co/nbeerbower/mistral-nemo-gutenberg-12B), [Lyra-Gutenberg-mistral-nemo-12B](https://huggingface.co/nbeerbower/Lyra-Gutenberg-mistral-nemo-12B)), the RPMax series, plus first-party [mistral-finetune](https://github.com/mistralai/mistral-finetune) tooling. Training fits on a single A100 80GB or even an RTX 4090 with QLoRA. The trade-off: smaller model means weaker form-following on metrically strict poetry, so it's the right pick _if_ `ground.exe`'s poetic identity is voice/imagery rather than form. Use Llama 3.3 70B if it's the latter.

**Workflow notes (all three):** HuggingFace fine-tuning is fully viable for all three. Recommend `axolotl` or `LlamaFactory` (which [supports 100+ models including Qwen3, Llama 3.3, Mistral Nemo](https://github.com/hiyouga/LlamaFactory)) over hand-rolled Trainer scripts — both ship pre-tested QLoRA + LoRA configs that survive creative-writing data without instruction-following collapse. Budget realistically: ~$50–200 in compute per training run on RunPod/Together for 14B models, $200–800 for 70B QLoRA, plus dataset curation time which will dominate.

I'm explicitly _not_ recommending Mistral Large 2 — its Mistral Research License blocks commercial deployment without a paid licence ([mistralai docs](https://docs.mistral.ai/models/mistral-large-2-0-24-07)), which is a non-starter for a public art project. I'm also not recommending DeepSeek R1/V3.2 as the base despite their permissive licence: R1 is a reasoning model whose long chain-of-thought outputs are awkward to fine-tune away from, and V3.2 is so large (671B MoE) that the fine-tuning practicality drops below "weekend project".

## 6. Wildcards — 1-2 unconventional picks

### Wildcard 1 — Verse by Verse-style per-poet small models

Train 3–5 small (1B-class) models on a single 20th/21st-century poet each, à la [Verse by Verse](https://blog.google/technology/ai/verse-by-verse/) (which fine-tuned per-poet on Whitman, Dickinson, Poe). Suggested base: **Qwen3-0.6B or Qwen3-1.7B** — already proven to handle creative writing fine-tunes ([Shriharsh/qwen3-0.6b-creative-writing](https://huggingface.co/Shriharsh/qwen3-0.6b-creative-writing)) and trains in 30 minutes on a single 4090. Each model is a "ghost" of one poet. Pit them against `ground.exe` (Halim's main model) and against each other in voting rounds. Project-wise, this gives the _Singulars_ installation a haunted, multi-voice quality that a single frontier model can't.

### Wildcard 2 — RAG over Halim's own corpus + a strong base

PoetRAT-style ([sciencedirect.com](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1568494626005831)) — keep the base model small/cheap (DeepSeek V3.2 chat at $0.28/M is plenty), but build a Prosody Knowledge Base from formal-verse references and a retrieval index over (a) every poem ever submitted to a Singulars performance, (b) Halim's published work, (c) a hand-curated canon. Generation prompts pull 3–5 nearest neighbours from the index and condition on them. PoetRAT reports 95%+ format-accuracy gains and 20–30% rhyme-accuracy gains _without retraining_, which is the most cost-effective intervention in the literature. Also: it gives `ground.exe` an internal mythology — every machine poem is in dialogue with the corpus of all prior performances. That's a strong artistic premise even if benchmark scores plateau.
